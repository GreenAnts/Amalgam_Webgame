Amalgam

Growing Amalgam into a Game Worth Playing

I first designed Amalgam over ten years ago, back in high school, inspired by Pai Sho from Avatar: The Last Airbender. Since then, the game has evolved dramatically—its rules have been rewritten, mechanics refined, and the design has grown far beyond its original form.

I’ve returned to the project on and off over the years, and this release represents the most complete version yet: a fully playable game designed not just to be finished, but to support a community; one that can grow, experiment, and help shape Amalgam’s future.

The project is fully open source [GPL-3.0 for the code & CC-BY 4.0 for the game content] to ensure Amalgam remains free, transparent, and community-driven. By releasing it this way, anyone is free to study the rules, modify mechanics, experiment with new variations, or create entirely new rulebooks. The goal isn’t a fixed, finished product, but a living game shaped by the people who play it.

If this kind of evolving, abstract strategy game interests you, I invite you to take part. Amalgam cannot grow in isolation—but with a community behind it, it has the chance to adapt, mature, and become something truly worth playing.
